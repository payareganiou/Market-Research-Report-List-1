<p><p>The polio vaccination market comprises two main types: Oral Polio Vaccine (OPV) and Inactivated Polio Vaccine (IPV). OPV is a live attenuated vaccine administered orally, offering ease of administration and potential for herd immunity; it is commonly used in mass immunization campaigns, especially in developing regions. Conversely, IPV is an inactivated vaccine given via injection, providing individual immunity without risk of vaccine-derived poliovirus. The choice between OPV and IPV often depends on regional epidemiology and public health strategies.</p></p>
