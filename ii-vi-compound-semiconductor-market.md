<p><p>The II-VI compound semiconductor market encompasses various deposition technologies essential for fabricating these materials. Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) facilitates high-quality thin films, while Molecular Beam Epitaxy (MBE) enables precise control over layer thickness and composition. Hydride Vapor Phase Epitaxy (HVPE) is noted for its efficiency in growing thick layers, whereas Metal-Organic Vapor Phase Epitaxy (MOVPE) excels in manufacturing complex structures. Liquid Phase Epitaxy (LPE) is a traditional technique known for bulk crystal growth, catering to diverse applications in optoelectronics and high-frequency devices.</p></p>
