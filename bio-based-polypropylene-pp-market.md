<p><p>Bio-based polypropylene (PP) is an eco-friendly alternative to conventional polypropylene, derived from renewable resources. The market for bio-based PP is primarily segmented into three types: sugars, starch, and lignocellulosic biomass. Sugars are processed through fermentation to produce bio-based PP, while starch is extracted from crops like corn or potatoes, utilized for conversion processes. Lignocellulosic biomass, derived from plant materials such as wood and agricultural residues, offers a sustainable source for bio-based PP, reducing reliance on fossil fuels and minimizing environmental impact.</p></p>
