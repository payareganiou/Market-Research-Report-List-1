<p><ul><li>Chemical Vapor Deposition</li><li>Liquid Impregnation Process</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Carbon Fiber-Reinforced Carbon (CFRC) market is primarily categorized into two main manufacturing processes: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Liquid Impregnation Process. CVD involves depositing carbon fibers into a matrix through gas reactions, resulting in high-density, high-performance composites suitable for extreme environments. In contrast, the Liquid Impregnation Process entails saturating carbon fibers with a liquid resin or polymer, allowing for more accessible production of complex shapes. Each method offers distinct advantages based on application requirements and material properties.</p></p>
