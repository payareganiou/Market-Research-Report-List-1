<p><p>The genomic biomarker market includes three primary types: protein markers, nucleic acid markers, and other markers. Protein markers are based on the presence or levels of proteins, serving as indicators for disease states or treatment responses. Nucleic acid markers involve DNA and RNA alterations, aiding in diagnosis and prognosis by identifying genetic mutations or expression changes. Other markers encompass a variety of emerging biomolecules and metabolites, providing additional insights into biological processes and disease mechanisms, thus enhancing personalized medicine approaches.</p></p>
