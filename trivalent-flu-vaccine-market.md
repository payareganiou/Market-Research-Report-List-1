<p><p>The trivalent flu vaccine market comprises three primary delivery methods: intramuscular injection, nasal spray, and intradermal shot. Intramuscular injections are the most common and involve administering the vaccine deep into the muscle, ensuring effective absorption. Nasal sprays provide a needle-free alternative, allowing for easy self-administration and appeal to those averse to needles. Intradermal shots are delivered into the skin's outer layer, using a smaller needle, aimed at providing a lower dose while maintaining efficacy, catering to diverse patient preferences.</p></p>
